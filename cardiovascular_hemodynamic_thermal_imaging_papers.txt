PAPER 1: Hemodynamic Shock Prediction from Thermal Images (Foundational ML Paper)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Predicting Hemodynamic Shock from Thermal Images using Machine Learning
Authors: Nagori A, Dhingra LS, Bhatnagar A, Lodha R, Tavpritesh S
Year: 2019
Journal: Scientific Reports (Nature), 9, Article 91
DOI: 10.1038/s41598-018-36586-8
Link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-018-36586-8

Key Findings:
- Demonstrated an affordable, non-invasive, non-contact thermal imaging system for
  automated shock detection and prediction in a pediatric ICU of a tertiary care hospital.
- Used 539 thermal images (253 with concomitant intra-arterial blood pressure) and
  histogram of oriented gradient (HOG) features for ML-based region-of-interest
  segmentation achieving 96% agreement with a human expert.
- Center-to-periphery temperature difference + pulse rate fed into a generalized linear
  mixed-effects model achieved AUC of 75% at 0 hours (classification), 77% at 3 hours,
  and 69% at 12 hours (prediction).

PAPER 2: Deep Learning on Thermal Videos for Shock Prediction (Advanced Follow-up)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Early Prediction of Hemodynamic Shock in Pediatric Intensive Care Units
       With Deep Learning on Thermal Videos
Authors: Vats V, Nagori A, Singh P, Dutt R, Bandhey H, Wason R, Lodha R, Sethi T
Year: 2022
Journal: Frontiers in Physiology, 13, Article 862411
DOI: 10.3389/fphys.2022.862411
Link: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/physiology/articles/10.3389/fphys.2022.862411/full

Key Findings:
- Advanced noncontact thermal imaging for continuous hemodynamic shock monitoring
  using 103,936 frames from 406 videos recorded longitudinally on 22 pediatric patients.
- Deep learning (LSTM models) extracted Center-to-Peripheral Difference (CPD) from
  thermal videos and predicted shock status up to 6 hours in advance.
- Best AUC-ROC of 0.81 +/- 0.06 and AUC-PR of 0.78 +/- 0.05 at 5-hour prediction
  horizon, providing sufficient lead time for clinical intervention.
- Used low-cost Seek Thermal Compact camera (resolution 206x156 thermal pixels),
  demonstrating feasibility with frugal open-source technology.

PAPER 3: Multi-Model Thermal Gradient Shock Prediction (Latest ML Approach)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Predicting Shock in Pediatric Patients Through Thermal Gradients and
       Machine Learning: A Multi-Model Approach
Authors: Espinoza Caro RA, Fajardo J
Year: 2026
Journal: IEEE Latin America Transactions, Volume 24, No. 2, pp. 144-152
DOI: 10.1109/TLA.2026.10224 (approximate)
Link: https://latamt.ieeer9.org/index.php/transactions/article/view/10224

Key Findings:
- Evaluated thermal gradients (abdomen-to-foot temperature difference) as non-invasive
  physiological markers for predicting shock in pediatric patients.
- Compared multiple ML models; best approach achieved AUC of 0.84, with sensitivity
  of 0.90 and specificity of 0.74.
- Demonstrated that combining thermal gradients with conventional vital signs (pulse rate,
  age) enhances early risk stratification and supports clinical decision-making in PICU.

PAPER 4: Hemodynamic Assessment Post-Cardiac Surgery via IRT (Cardiac Focus)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Hemodynamic assessment in children after cardiac surgery: A pilot study
       on the value of infrared thermography
Authors: Bridier A, Shcherbakova M, Kawaguchi A, Poirier N, Said C, Noumeir R, Jouvet P
Year: 2023
Journal: Frontiers in Pediatrics, 11, Article 1083962
DOI: 10.3389/fped.2023.1083962
Link: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/pediatrics/articles/10.3389/fped.2023.1083962/full

Key Findings:
- Assessed correlation between thermal gradient obtained by IRT and oxygen extraction
  ratio (O2ER) in 41 children (age 0-18) following cardiac surgery with cardiopulmonary bypass.
- Found a significant but weak correlation between thermal gradient (inner canthus of
  eye vs. peripheral temperature) and O2ER, suggesting potential for early detection
  of low cardiac output syndrome.
- Used FLIR One Pro thermal imaging camera; however, 46% of expected images were not
  usable due to heat sources, obstruction, or temperature artifacts -- highlighting
  practical challenges for clinical deployment.
- Concluded IRT is promising for multimodal post-cardiac surgery monitoring but needs
  validation in more severe patient populations.

PAPER 5: Thermal Imaging-Based CPTD for Neonatal NICU Monitoring
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Thermal imaging-based core peripheral temperature difference measurement
       for neonatal monitoring in the NICU
Authors: Zhang N, Song X, He J, Liang F, Yang J, Wang W
Year: 2025
Journal: Biomedical Optics Express, Volume 16, Issue 3, pp. 965-981
DOI: 10.1364/BOE.549693
Link: https://opg.optica.org/boe/abstract.cfm?uri=boe-16-3-965
PMC: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11919349/

Key Findings:
- First clinical showcase of using a thermal infrared (TIR) camera for continuous,
  non-contact, fully-automatic core-peripheral temperature difference (CPTD) monitoring
  of preterm infants in a hospital NICU.
- Leveraged deep learning to automatically identify chest-abdomen (core) and hand
  (peripheral) regions in neonatal TIR videos with average precision of 76% (core)
  and 85.5% (peripheral).
- Prospective study of 40 preterm infants; demonstrated CPTD as a key biomarker
  for hemodynamic status, with higher CPTD values correlating with circulatory disorders.
- Addresses critical gap: traditional spot thermometer CPTD measurement cannot provide
  continuous long-term automated monitoring.

PAPER 6: Scoping Review of Thermal Imaging in Neonatal Care (Review Paper)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Thermal imaging applications in neonatal care: a scoping review
Authors: Topalidou A, Ali N, Sekulic S, Downe S
Year: 2019
Journal: BMC Pregnancy and Childbirth, 19, Article 381
DOI: 10.1186/s12884-019-2533-y
Link: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12884-019-2533-y
PMC: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6814124/

Key Findings:
- Comprehensive scoping review of 21 studies (from 442 initial hits) on thermal imaging
  in neonatal care, with most studies having small cohorts (4-29 participants).
- Identified five themes: general thermal physiology; heat loss and respiratory
  monitoring; identification of internal pathologies (including NEC); other uses;
  and technical concerns.
- Highlighted that an infant who developed congestive heart failure demonstrated a
  cooler chest area on thermal imaging, suggesting screening potential for cardiac
  abnormalities.
- Concluded thermal imaging is reliable and non-invasive for continuous neonatal
  temperature monitoring but faces methodological challenges (incubators, radiant warmers).

PAPER 7: Neonatal Health Status Detection using IRT + Deep CNNs
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Health status detection of neonates using infrared thermography and deep
       convolutional neural networks
Authors: Ornek AH, Ceylan M, Ervural S
Year: 2019
Journal: Infrared Physics & Technology, 103, Article 103044
DOI: 10.1016/j.infrared.2019.103044
Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1350449519303123

Key Findings:
- First study to combine IRT with deep CNNs for automated neonatal health status
  classification (healthy vs. unhealthy) in a NICU setting.
- Used 380-30,400 thermal images from 38 neonates at Selcuk University NICU over one
  year; data augmentation improved classification accuracy from 84.5% to 90.9%.
- Demonstrated that diseases and infections cause detectable local temperature
  imbalances in neonates that can be captured by thermography and classified by AI,
  enabling early-stage disease detection.

PAPER 8: Thermoregulation and Thermography in Neonatal Physiology (Foundational)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Thermoregulation and Thermography in Neonatal Physiology and Disease
Authors: Abbas AK, Heimann K, Jergus K, Orlikowsky T, Leonhardt S
Year: 2012 (online 2011)
Journal: Biological Research for Nursing, 14(4), pp. 338-348
DOI: 10.1177/1099800411403467
Link: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3775585/

Key Findings:
- Foundational review of neonatal thermoregulation physiology and the role of infrared
  thermography for non-contact thermal assessment.
- Discussed how skin temperature patterns reflect underlying perfusion and hemodynamic
  status, with peripheral vasoconstriction detectable through thermal gradients.
- Established the scientific basis for using thermal imaging as a surrogate marker
  for cardiovascular and hemodynamic assessment in neonates.

PAPER 9: Infrared Thermography for Peripheral Perfusion in Septic Shock (Adult,
         Methodologically Relevant)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Infrared Thermography Imaging for Assessment of Peripheral Perfusion in
       Patients with Septic Shock
Authors: (Multiple authors)
Year: 2023
Journal: PMC (peer-reviewed)
DOI: See PMC link
Link: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10295664/

Key Findings:
- IRT allows non-invasive evaluation of temperature distribution over a larger body
  surface and permits evaluation of temperature gradients across extremities.
- Cool skin and large skin temperature gradients were associated with more severe organ
  failure and poor survival in septic shock patients.
- Methodology directly applicable to neonatal populations: activation of sympathetic
  nervous system during shock leads to peripheral vasoconstriction, reducing peripheral
  temperature while core temperature remains stable -- detectable by thermal camera.

PAPER 10: Capillary Refill Time Measurement Using Imaging Technology
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Measurement of Capillary Refill Time (CRT) in Healthy Subjects Using a
       Robotic Hand
Authors: Kerr E, Coleman S, McGinnity TM, Shepherd A
Year: 2018
Journal: IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR) Workshops,
         Salt Lake City, UT, pp. 1372-1379
DOI: N/A (Conference proceedings)
Link: https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content_cvpr_2018_workshops/papers/w27/Kerr_Measurement_of_Capillary_CVPR_2018_paper.pdf

Key Findings:
- Developed a novel algorithm for objective CRT measurement using RGB camera and
  biomimetic tactile fingertip sensors, applicable regardless of skin tone.
- CRT is a key indicator of cardiovascular status; being able to accurately assess
  peripheral perfusion could be life-saving in emergency settings.
- While this paper used RGB rather than thermal cameras, it establishes the framework
  for automated, objective CRT measurement -- thermal cameras could potentially offer
  superior contrast for tracking reperfusion dynamics.

PAPER 11: Emerging Innovations in Neonatal Non-Contact Monitoring (2024 Review)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Emerging innovations in neonatal monitoring: a comprehensive review of
       progress and potential for non-contact technologies
Authors: (Multiple authors)
Year: 2024
Journal: Frontiers in Pediatrics, 12, Article 1442753
DOI: 10.3389/fped.2024.1442753
Link: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/pediatrics/articles/10.3389/fped.2024.1442753/full

Key Findings:
- Comprehensive review covering non-contact technologies for neonatal monitoring
  including thermal infrared cameras for hemodynamic assessment.
- TIR cameras offer significant benefits for NICU monitoring: rapid non-contact
  temperature measurements, reduced cross-contamination risk, improved comfort,
  and support for continuous 24/7 monitoring.
- Neonates typically present with "cold shock" (elevated systemic vascular resistance,
  low cardiac output, cold extremities) -- peripheral vasoconstriction identifiable
  using center-to-peripheral temperature difference with thermal imaging.

KEY THEMES FOR NEOTHERM RELEVANCE
================================================================================

1. CONGENITAL HEART DISEASE SCREENING:
   - No dedicated published study on thermal imaging for CHD screening exists yet.
   - However, Topalidou (2019) noted a case where congestive heart failure produced
     a cooler chest area on thermal imaging.
   - Coarctation of the aorta produces upper-lower body temperature differences
     (due to reduced blood flow distal to coarctation) -- this is a known clinical
     sign but has NOT been studied with thermal cameras specifically. This represents
     a NOVEL research opportunity for NeoTherm.

2. HEMODYNAMIC SHOCK PREDICTION:
   - Most developed area with 3 progressive papers (Nagori 2019 -> Vats 2022 ->
     Espinoza 2026) showing improving ML performance (AUC 0.77 -> 0.81 -> 0.84).
   - Core-to-peripheral temperature difference (CPTD/CPD) is the key biomarker.
   - Low-cost cameras (Seek Thermal Compact ~$250) are sufficient.

3. CARDIAC OUTPUT ESTIMATION:
   - Bridier (2023) showed correlation between IRT thermal gradient and oxygen
     extraction ratio (proxy for cardiac output) post-cardiac surgery.
   - Weak but significant correlation suggests IRT alone insufficient -- needs
     multimodal integration.

4. CAPILLARY REFILL TIME:
   - Objective measurement methods exist (Kerr 2018) but thermal cameras have not
     been specifically validated for CRT in neonates. Another NOVEL opportunity.

5. PERIPHERAL PERFUSION / VASCULAR RESISTANCE:
   - Well-established that thermal imaging reflects peripheral perfusion status.
   - Zhang (2025) demonstrated automated continuous CPTD monitoring in NICU.
   - Directly applicable to NeoTherm's value proposition.
