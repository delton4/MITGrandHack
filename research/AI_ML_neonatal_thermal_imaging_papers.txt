1. DEEP LEARNING CLASSIFICATION OF NEONATAL HEALTH FROM THERMAL IMAGES
================================================================================

PAPER 1.1
----------
Title: Health Status Detection of Neonates Using Infrared Thermography and
       Deep Convolutional Neural Networks
Authors: Ornek AH, Ceylan M, Ervural S
Year: 2019
Journal: Infrared Physics & Technology (Elsevier)
DOI/Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1350449519303123

Key Findings:
- First study to combine IRT and deep CNNs for neonatal health status
  detection (healthy vs. unhealthy classification).
- Thermal images of 38 neonates (19 healthy, 19 unhealthy) captured at
  Selcuk University NICU over one year. Dataset augmented from 3,800 to
  30,400 images.
- Achieved 99.58% accuracy, 99.73% specificity, 99.43% sensitivity, and
  0.996 AUC. 15,159/15,200 healthy thermograms and 15,114/15,200 unhealthy
  thermograms were correctly classified.


PAPER 1.2
----------
Title: Medical Thermograms' Classification Using Deep Transfer Learning
       Models and Methods
Authors: Ornek AH, Ceylan M
Year: 2022
Journal: Multimedia Tools and Applications (Springer)
DOI/Link: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11042-021-11852-6

Key Findings:
- Compared nine pre-trained deep transfer learning models (VGG16, VGG19,
  Xception, ResNet101, ResNet50, Inceptionv3, InceptionResNetv2, MobileNet,
  DenseNet201) with two classification methods (softmax and SVM) for
  healthy/unhealthy neonatal classification.
- Best results: VGG19 + SVM achieved 100.00% specificity, sensitivity, and
  accuracy on the neonatal thermogram dataset.
- Demonstrated that transfer learning effectively addresses the challenge of
  limited neonatal thermal image data.


PAPER 1.3
----------
Title: CodCAM: A New Ensemble Visual Explanation for Classification of
       Medical Thermal Images
Authors: Ornek AH, Ceylan M
Year: 2023
Journal: Quantitative InfraRed Thermography Journal, Vol. 21, No. 3
DOI/Link: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/17686733.2023.2167459

Key Findings:
- Trained CNN model to classify neonatal thermograms as healthy/unhealthy,
  achieving 99.91% train accuracy, 99.47% validation accuracy, and 99.82%
  test accuracy.
- Proposed CodCAM, a new ensemble Explainable AI (XAI) method combining
  GradCAM, GradCAM++, LayerCAM, and EigenCAM to visualize which regions of
  neonatal thermograms drive classification decisions.
- Enables medical specialists to understand which body regions influence the
  CNN's decision, building clinical trust in AI models.


PAPER 1.4
----------
Title: A New Approach for Super-Resolution and Classification Applications
       on Neonatal Thermal Images
Authors: Ornek AH, Ceylan M
Year: 2023
Journal: Quantitative InfraRed Thermography Journal, Vol. 21, No. 3
DOI/Link: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/17686733.2023.2179282

Key Findings:
- Applied super-resolution models combined with transfer learning
  (ResNet101, Xception) for neonatal thermal image classification.
- Super-resolution preprocessing increased healthy/unhealthy classification
  accuracy by approximately 10% compared to low-resolution inputs.
- ResNet101 yielded the most successful classification results among the
  tested architectures.

2. OBJECT DETECTION / ROI SEGMENTATION IN NEONATAL THERMAL IMAGES
================================================================================

PAPER 2.1
----------
Title: A Method for Improving Semantic Segmentation Using Thermographic
       Images in Infants
Authors: Hesse N, Lyra S, Leonhardt S, et al.
Year: 2022 (published online Jan 2022)
Journal: BMC Medical Imaging
DOI/Link: https://bmcmedimaging.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/s12880-021-00730-0

Key Findings:
- Developed semantic segmentation for automatic ROI setting in infant
  thermal images, classifying pixels into five classes: head, body, arms,
  legs, and "other."
- U-Net GAN with Self-Attention (SA) achieved best results: 93.5% accuracy
  and 70.4% mean Intersection over Union (mIoU), outperforming standard
  U-Net (92.9% accuracy, 64.5% mIoU).
- Eliminates the need for manual ROI selection, making thermal analysis
  practical for continuous clinical monitoring.


PAPER 2.2
----------
Title: Multi-Modal Body Part Segmentation of Infants Using Deep Learning
Authors: Hesse N, Lyra S, Leonhardt S, et al.
Year: 2023
Journal: BioMedical Engineering OnLine (Springer)
DOI/Link: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12938-023-01092-0

Key Findings:
- Compared three U-Net-based neural networks for infant body part
  segmentation using RGB, LWIR (thermal), and a hybrid fusion approach.
  Dataset: 600 visible + 600 thermography images from 20 infant recordings.
- Fusion model achieved best results with mIoU of 0.85, closely followed by
  RGB-only model; thermal-only model achieved mIoU of 0.75.
- First study to report multi-modal approach for infant body part
  segmentation, combining cross-modality transfer learning with classical
  augmentation strategies.

3. MULTI-MODAL FUSION (THERMAL + RGB + DEPTH) FOR NEONATAL MONITORING
================================================================================

PAPER 3.1
----------
Title: Camera Fusion for Real-Time Temperature Monitoring of Neonates
       Using Deep Learning
Authors: Lyra S, Mayer L, Ou L, Chen D, Timms P, Schäfer A, Leonhardt S,
         Antink CH
Year: 2022
Journal: Medical & Biological Engineering & Computing (Springer)
DOI/Link: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11517-022-02561-9

Key Findings:
- Presented a deep learning approach for real-time neonatal temperature
  monitoring using multi-camera fusion (RGB + infrared thermography).
- Used trained DL keypoint detector for body landmark prediction in RGB
  images, with image registration to transfer landmarks to thermographic
  recordings for local body surface temperature extraction.
- Demonstrated extraction of central-peripheral temperature differences
  (cpTD) to support early detection of clinical symptoms such as neonatal
  sepsis.


PAPER 3.2
----------
Title: Sensor Fusion for the Robust Detection of Facial Regions of
       Neonates Using Neural Networks
Authors: Lyra S, et al.
Year: 2023
Journal: Sensors (MDPI), Vol. 23, No. 10, 4910
DOI/Link: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10223875/

Key Findings:
- Novel indirect fusion approach combining thermal camera, RGB camera, and
  3D Time-of-Flight (ToF) camera, tailored for short distances inside
  neonatal incubators.
- Evaluated RetinaNet and YOLOv3 architectures for neonatal face detection.
  First study to train a neural network with fusion data of neonates,
  increasing data efficiency by 66%.
- ToF camera addresses the problem of varying neonate sizes affecting
  calibration distance in direct thermal-RGB fusion.


PAPER 3.3
----------
Title: Neonatal Pose Estimation in the Unaltered Clinical Environment
       with Fusion of RGB, Depth and IR Images
Authors: Sherwood D, et al.
Year: 2025
Journal: npj Digital Medicine (Nature)
DOI/Link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41746-025-01929-z

Key Findings:
- Clinical study collecting RGB, depth, and IR video from 24 neonates with
  no modifications to clinical care, using Azure Kinect camera at 30 fps.
- Late fusion method for torso keypoint localization achieved best average
  precision score of 0.811.
- Found that chest covering and side-lying decrease performance by 0.15 and
  0.10 respectively, occurring 50% and 44% of the time — highlighting the
  importance of validation in realistic clinical conditions.

4. TRANSFER LEARNING APPROACHES FOR NEONATAL THERMAL IMAGE ANALYSIS
================================================================================

PAPER 4.1
----------
Title: Deep Learning Based Super Resolution and Classification
       Applications for Neonatal Thermal Images
Authors: Ornek AH, Ceylan M
Year: 2021
Journal: Traitement du Signal (IIETA), Vol. 38, No. 5
DOI/Link: https://www.iieta.org/journals/ts/paper/10.18280/ts.380511

Key Findings:
- Designed a CNN-based classifier and applied transfer learning using
  ResNet101 and Xception pre-trained models.
- ResNet101 produced the most successful classification results for
  neonatal healthy/unhealthy detection.
- Super-resolution preprocessing improved classification success by ~10%
  over low-resolution images.


PAPER 4.2
----------
Title: Convolutional Neural Networks-Based Approach to Detect Neonatal
       Respiratory System Anomalies with Limited Thermal Image
Authors: Ornek AH, Ceylan M, Ervural S
Year: 2021
Journal: Traitement du Signal (IIETA), Vol. 38, No. 2
DOI/Link: https://www.iieta.org/journals/ts/paper/10.18280/ts.380222

Key Findings:
- Used CNNs to classify neonatal respiratory system abnormalities vs.
  cardiovascular and abdominal diseases from thermal images.
- Classification accuracy improved from 84.5% to 90.9% with 4x data
  augmentation.
- Demonstrated feasibility of disease-specific classification (not just
  healthy/unhealthy) from neonatal thermograms.


PAPER 4.3
----------
Title: Thermogram Classification Using Deep Siamese Network for Neonatal
       Disease Detection with Limited Data
Authors: Ervural S, Ceylan M
Year: 2022
Journal: Quantitative InfraRed Thermography Journal, Vol. 19, No. 5
DOI/Link: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/17686733.2021.2010379

Key Findings:
- Applied Siamese neural networks (meta-learning / one-shot learning) for
  multi-class neonatal disease classification from thermograms with very
  limited data.
- Achieved disease-specific accuracies: 99.4% for infection diseases, 96.4%
  for oesophageal atresia, 97.4% for intestinal atresia, and 94.02% for
  necrotising enterocolitis.
- Siamese networks effectively address the fundamental challenge of creating
  large thermogram databases given newborn sensitivity.

5. REAL-TIME PROCESSING SYSTEMS FOR NEONATAL THERMAL MONITORING
================================================================================

PAPER 5.1
----------
Title: Non-Contact Respiratory Rate Monitoring Using Thermal and Visible
       Imaging: A Pilot Study on Neonates
Authors: Maurya L, et al.
Year: 2023
Journal: Journal of Clinical Monitoring and Computing (Springer)
DOI/Link: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10877-022-00945-8

Key Findings:
- Integrated visible (RGB) and thermal (T) image sequences for ROI
  selection and tracking for breathing rate extraction. Deep-learning-based
  tracking-by-detection detects ROI in RGB images, mapped to thermal images
  via RGB-T image registration.
- Average error of 1.5 bpm for neonates compared to contact belt reference;
  0.1 bpm for healthy adults in steady conditions.
- Bland-Altman analysis confirmed good agreement, providing evidence for
  use as a contactless respiratory rate monitoring method.


PAPER 5.2
----------
Title: Non-Contact Automatic Vital Signs Monitoring of Infants in a
       Neonatal Intensive Care Unit Based on Neural Networks
Authors: Chaichulee S, et al.
Year: 2021
Journal: Journal of Imaging (MDPI), Vol. 7, No. 8, 122
DOI/Link: https://www.mdpi.com/2313-433X/7/8/122

Key Findings:
- Developed neural network-based system for automatic non-contact vital
  signs monitoring (heart rate, respiratory rate) in NICU using camera data.
- Demonstrated feasibility of continuous, cable-free monitoring that reduces
  risks associated with contact sensors on premature neonatal skin.
- Used CNN-based approaches for automatic detection and tracking of infant
  body regions from video feeds.

6. PREDICTIVE MODELS COMBINING THERMAL DATA WITH OTHER VITAL SIGNS
================================================================================

PAPER 6.1
----------
Title: AI-Guided Computing Insights into a Thermostat Monitoring Neonatal
       Intensive Care Unit (NICU)
Authors: Khalid R, et al.
Year: 2023
Journal: Sensors (MDPI), Vol. 23, No. 9, 4492
DOI/Link: https://www.mdpi.com/1424-8220/23/9/4492

Key Findings:
- Used CFD (computational fluid dynamics) simulations and machine learning
  to predict thermal performance in NICU incubators in real time.
- ML model learns patterns in temperature fluctuations to feed information
  into thermostat systems for automated environmental control.
- Demonstrated that AI can handle large NICU datasets and identify patterns
  in sensor readings for improved neonatal thermal management.


PAPER 6.2
----------
Title: Towards Intelligent NICU Monitoring: Real-Time Anomaly Detection
       in Vital Signs Using Deep Learning Models
Authors: [Multiple authors]
Year: 2024
Journal: Proceedings of the 2024 7th Artificial Intelligence and Cloud
         Computing Conference (ACM)
DOI/Link: https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3719384.3719467

Key Findings:
- Compared LSTM, GRU, and 1D-CNN deep learning architectures for real-time
  anomaly detection in NICU vital signs (heart rate, respiratory rate,
  temperature, oxygen saturation, blood pressure).
- LSTM model achieved best results: 94.1% accuracy, 93.2% precision, 90.5%
  recall, and 3.2% false alarm rate.
- Designed for real-time deployment to support clinical decision-making in
  neonatal intensive care.

7. SYNTHETIC DATA GENERATION / DATA AUGMENTATION FOR NEONATAL THERMAL
================================================================================

PAPER 7.1
----------
Title: Data Augmentation of Neonatal Thermal Images Using Deep Learning
Authors: Ornek AH, Ceylan M
Year: 2021
Journal: IEEE Conference Publication (IEEE TIPTEKNO)
DOI/Link: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/9579769/

Key Findings:
- Used DCGAN (Deep Convolutional GAN) to generate synthetic neonatal
  thermal images that resemble original images yet provide greater diversity
  than traditional augmentation methods.
- Traditional augmentation (brightness, color, noise, resolution changes)
  produces copies with limited diversity; DCGAN generates truly novel
  variations.
- Addresses the fundamental challenge that obtaining neonatal thermal images
  is costly and constrained by patient/hospital conditions.


PAPER 7.2
----------
Title: Conditional Generative Adversarial Networks for Data Augmentation
       of a Neonatal Image Dataset
Authors: Hesse N, Lyra S, et al.
Year: 2023
Journal: Sensors (MDPI), Vol. 23, No. 2, 999
DOI/Link: https://www.mdpi.com/1424-8220/23/2/999

Key Findings:
- Adapted Pix2PixHD (conditional GAN) as an image-to-image translation
  network to synthesize photorealistic neonatal images from edge detection
  frames.
- Trained on European infant dataset, tested on Indian NICU dataset to
  explore cross-population augmentation with diverse skin tones. 28% of
  generated images rated as more realistic than manually augmented images.
- Achieved FID score of 103.82, demonstrating promising results for cGAN-
  based augmentation of highly limited neonatal datasets.


PAPER 7.3
----------
Title: Classification of Neonatal Diseases with Limited Thermal Image Data
Authors: Ervural S, Ceylan M
Year: 2022
Journal: Multimedia Tools and Applications (Springer)
DOI/Link: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11042-021-11391-0

Key Findings:
- Proposed classification model combining data augmentation and AI for
  neonatal disease detection with limited data (20 images per neonate from
  44 neonates).
- Best classification accuracy of 94% achieved; 89% when testing with
  images from neonates not used in training.
- Addressed the critical challenge that early diagnosis from limited data
  is vital due to high neonatal mortality rates and imaging constraints.

9. RECENT AI + THERMAL IMAGING FOR NEONATAL CARE (2024-2025)
================================================================================

PAPER 9.1
----------
Title: Advancing Newborn Care: Precise Time of Birth Detection Using
       AI-Driven Thermal Imaging with Adaptive Normalization
Authors: Garcia-Torres J, Meinich-Bache O, et al.
Year: 2025
Journal: Computers in Biology and Medicine (Elsevier)
DOI/Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0010482525000769

Key Findings:
- First AI-driven Time of Birth (ToB) detector combining AI with thermal
  imaging. Three-step approach: (1) adaptive normalization via Gaussian
  Mixture Models, (2) AI model for newborn detection in thermal frames,
  (3) post-processing for ToB estimation.
- Achieved 88.1% precision and 89.3% recall for newborn detection in
  thermal frames, with median ToB deviation of 2.7 seconds from manual
  annotations.
- Privacy-preserving approach (GDPR-compliant) leveraging newborns' higher
  skin temperature for identification without identifiable imagery.


PAPER 9.2
----------
Title: Two-Stream Thermal Imaging Fusion for Enhanced Time of Birth
       Detection in Neonatal Care
Authors: Garcia-Torres J, et al.
Year: 2025
Journal: arXiv preprint / IEEE Conference Publication
DOI/Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.03244

Key Findings:
- Novel two-stream fusion system combining static image analysis and
  dynamic video analysis to detect ToB from thermal recordings in delivery
  rooms and operating theaters.
- Achieved 95.7% precision and 84.8% recall in detecting birth within
  short video clips.
- Captures richer spatiotemporal features by integrating static and dynamic
  streams for more robust birth detection.


PAPER 9.3
----------
Title: Emerging Innovations in Neonatal Monitoring: A Comprehensive Review
       of Progress and Potential for Non-Contact Technologies
Authors: [Multiple authors]
Year: 2024
Journal: Frontiers in Pediatrics
DOI/Link: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/pediatrics/articles/10.3389/fped.2024.1442753/full

Key Findings:
- Comprehensive review of non-contact monitoring technologies for neonates,
  covering thermal imaging, RGB cameras, radar, and other modalities.
- CNNs are identified as essential for advancing non-contact monitoring,
  particularly for processing image data from thermal and visible cameras.
- Highlights challenges including need for large annotated datasets, "black
  box" nature of deep learning models, and validation in realistic clinical
  scenarios.


PAPER 9.4
----------
Title: Next Generation of Non-Contact and Wireless Vital Sign Monitoring
       Technology in the Neonatal Intensive Care Unit: A Systematic Review
Authors: [Multiple authors]
Year: 2025
Journal: Pediatric Research (Nature)
DOI/Link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41390-025-04469-0

Key Findings:
- Systematic review of 60 studies (Jan 2014 - Aug 2024): 43 non-contact
  and 17 wireless sensor studies for neonatal vital sign monitoring.
- Thermal imaging used for respiratory rate monitoring (less affected by
  visual obstructions than RGB cameras) and body temperature monitoring.
- Identified the integration of multiple camera modalities (thermal + RGB)
  with deep learning as a key emerging trend for comprehensive neonatal
  monitoring.

   - Clinical pilot studies with neonates


================================================================================
TECHNOLOGY LANDSCAPE SUMMARY
================================================================================

ARCHITECTURES USED:
- Classification: Custom CNNs, VGG16/19, ResNet50/101, Xception,
  InceptionV3, InceptionResNetV2, MobileNet, DenseNet201
- Segmentation: U-Net, U-Net GAN, U-Net GAN + Self-Attention
- Detection: RetinaNet, YOLOv3, keypoint detectors
- Few-shot learning: Siamese networks
- Data generation: DCGAN, Pix2PixHD (cGAN)
- Time-series: LSTM, GRU, 1D-CNN
- Fusion: Late fusion, indirect fusion (with ToF depth)
- XAI: GradCAM, GradCAM++, LayerCAM, EigenCAM, CodCAM

KEY CHALLENGES IDENTIFIED:
1. Very limited neonatal thermal datasets (ethical/practical constraints)
2. High variability in clinical conditions (covering, positioning)
3. Need for real-time processing at the bedside
4. "Black box" nature of deep learning limits clinical trust
5. Cross-population generalization (skin tone, body size variation)
6. Validation in realistic, uncontrolled clinical environments
7. Integration with existing NICU clinical workflows

EMERGING TRENDS (2024-2025):
1. Privacy-preserving thermal-only approaches (GDPR-compliant)
2. Multi-modal fusion (RGB + thermal + depth) for robust monitoring
3. Explainable AI (XAI) methods for clinical interpretability
4. Synthetic data generation via GANs for dataset augmentation
5. Pose estimation and activity monitoring beyond temperature
6. Integration with delivery room care (Time of Birth detection)
7. Systematic reviews synthesizing the growing evidence base
