# Full Competitive Landscape: Post-Operative Deterioration Detection & Monitoring

**Date:** March 2026
**Scope:** All companies and products doing early detection of postoperative deterioration, sepsis, or surgical complications -- including continuous monitoring, AI/ML early warning, wearables, and remote monitoring. NOT limited to thermal imaging.

---

## MARKET OVERVIEW

- **Patient monitoring devices market:** $50.5B (2025), projected $134.6B by 2035 (CAGR 7.73%)
- **AI-enabled patient deterioration EWS market:** $0.8B (2025), growing at 20.5% CAGR through 2033
- **Sepsis diagnostics market:** $825M (2025), growing at 11% CAGR through 2035
- **AI-enhanced wearables market:** Growing at 25.5% CAGR from 2025-2030
- **RPM landscape:** Undergoing significant consolidation; cellular-enabled devices becoming standard

**The core problem:** On general surgical wards, vital signs are checked every 4-6 hours by nurses ("spot checks"). Between checks, deterioration goes undetected. 80% of cardiac arrests on general floors have detectable warning signs 6-8 hours beforehand. This is a massive, well-recognized gap.

## CATEGORY 1: CONTINUOUS MONITORING SYSTEMS FOR GENERAL/SURGICAL WARDS

### 1.1 Masimo -- Patient SafetyNet (In-Hospital) + SafetyNet (Remote)

**What it does:**
- **Patient SafetyNet:** Supplemental alarm notification system for general wards. Continuous SpO2 and pulse rate monitoring via Masimo SET pulse oximetry. Sends alerts to clinicians' mobile devices. Monitors up to 500 patients from central view station.
- **Masimo SafetyNet (telehealth):** Cloud-based remote monitoring platform for post-discharge patients. Uses sensors + smartphones. HIPAA-compliant.
- **Masimo W1 Medical Watch:** FDA-cleared wearable connected to SafetyNet telemonitoring system.

**FDA status:** Multiple 510(k) clearances. W1 watch cleared 2024 for SafetyNet connectivity. Radius VSM cleared 2023.

**Revenue/Funding:** Public company (MASI). 2024 revenue: $2.09B total, $1.395B healthcare segment (9% growth). Projects 8-11% healthcare growth in 2025.

**Hospital adoption:** Dominant player. Primary pulse oximetry at ALL 10 top U.S. hospitals (2024 Newsweek ranking). Used on >200 million patients globally. Dartmouth-Hitchcock expanded to 200+ inpatient beds across all med-surg units.

**Postop relevance:** Direct competitor. Study showed patients discharged with SafetyNet after total joint arthroplasty had significantly lower readmission rates. Calculated potential savings of $800K per 1,000 TJA surgeries. Saratoga Hospital reduced ICU transfers by 63% and eliminated codes in orthopedic unit (from 3-4/year to zero).

**Competitive threat level: VERY HIGH** -- This is the dominant incumbent in general ward continuous monitoring.

---

### 1.2 GE HealthCare -- Portrait Mobile + CARESCAPE Platform

**What it does:**
- **Portrait Mobile:** Smartphone-sized wireless patient-worn monitoring device. Continuous respiration rate, SpO2, pulse rate. Dual-vector respiration rate measurement. Patient remains fully mobile/untethered.
- **CARESCAPE platform:** Enterprise-wide monitoring from ICU to general ward. Supports up to 7 EWS protocols (MEWS, NEWS2). Integration with Medtronic for expanded parameters.

**FDA status:** Portrait Mobile received 510(k) clearance August 2023. First-of-its-kind wireless monitoring for general ward deterioration detection.

**Revenue/Funding:** Public company (GEHC). $19.6B total revenue (2024). Patient monitoring is one segment.

**Hospital adoption:** Global footprint. CARESCAPE deployed across ICU, PACU, med-surg, general floors. Partnership with Medtronic for expanded capabilities.

**Postop relevance:** Portrait Mobile specifically designed for post-surgical recovery and post-ICU discharge patients. Eliminates traditional tethers so patients can ambulate (key for postop recovery).

**Competitive threat level: VERY HIGH** -- Major incumbent with specific postop focus.

---

### 1.3 Philips -- IntelliVue Guardian Solution + Capsule Technologies

**What it does:**
- **IntelliVue Guardian:** Automated early warning scoring system. Acquires vital signs automatically, calculates EWS (MEWS/PEWS configurable), alerts clinicians. Integrates with wearable biosensors.
- **Capsule Technologies (acquired):** Vendor-neutral medical device integration platform. Collects data from ANY manufacturer's devices (GE, Medtronic, etc.) and routes to EMR. Automated EWS calculation at bedside.

**FDA status:** FDA-cleared. Multiple devices in the ecosystem.

**Revenue/Funding:** Public company (PHG). $19.5B healthcare revenue (2024).

**Hospital adoption:** Saratoga Hospital: 63% reduction in ICU transfers, eliminated codes on orthopedic floor. Lakeland Health integrated IntelliVue Guardian with automated EWS.

**Postop relevance:** Direct competition for ward-based continuous monitoring with automated deterioration alerts. Capsule's vendor-neutral approach means it can overlay on existing hospital infrastructure.

**Competitive threat level: VERY HIGH** -- One of the "big 3" monitoring incumbents.

---

### 1.4 Hillrom/Baxter -- EarlySense Contact-Free Monitoring

**What it does:** Under-mattress piezoelectric sensor that continuously monitors heart rate and respiratory rate without touching the patient. Alerts clinicians to deterioration. Integrated into Centrella Smart+ bed. Measures >100 times per minute.

**FDA status:** 510(k) cleared (K223246 under Baxter Healthcare).

**Revenue/Funding:** Hillrom acquired EarlySense's contact-free monitoring tech for $30M cash + milestone payments + equity in 2020. Hillrom subsequently acquired by Baxter International for $12.5B.

**Hospital adoption:** Deployed across Baxter/Hillrom hospital bed install base (one of the largest globally).

**Postop relevance:** Ideal for surgical patients who may not tolerate wearables. Zero patient compliance burden. Detects respiratory depression (key opioid-related postop risk).

**Competitive threat level: HIGH** -- Leverages massive existing hospital bed install base.

---

### 1.5 Sotera Digital Health -- ViSi Mobile

**What it does:** Wearable continuous vital signs monitoring: BP (cuff-less beat-to-beat), SpO2, respiration, pulse, ECG, posture, arrhythmia detection. Machine learning from millions of hours of patient data to recognize deterioration while minimizing alarm burden.

**FDA status:** 510(k) cleared (2012, updated versions since). CE marked.

**Revenue/Funding:** ~$22M annual revenue (2024). Private company.

**Hospital adoption:** Scripps Health, Intermountain Healthcare (22 hospitals, 185 clinics -- upgraded from pilot to full partnership).

**Postop relevance:** Specifically designed for general care and post-surgical areas. Provides ICU-level monitoring on the general ward. Continuous cuff-less blood pressure is a key differentiator (hemodynamic instability detection).

**Competitive threat level: MEDIUM-HIGH** -- Strong product but smaller scale than big 3.

## CATEGORY 2: WEARABLE VITAL SIGN MONITORS FOR POSTOP PATIENTS

### 2.1 VitalConnect -- VitalPatch Biosensor

**What it does:** Disposable chest-worn biosensor patch. Measures 8 biometrics continuously: HR, ECG, HRV, R-R interval, respiratory rate, skin temperature, activity/steps, posture. Industry's smallest and lightest wearable biosensor.

**FDA status:** 5 separate 510(k) clearances. Cleared for 120-hour (5-day) continuous wear -- covers average hospital stay of 4.5 days.

**Revenue/Funding:** $383M total funding raised. $100M debt round Feb 2025. Series F $30M+ (2023). Revenue tripled 2021-2022, nearly doubled again 2023.

**Hospital adoption:** Hundreds of thousands of biosensors deployed. Used at Brigham and Women's, Hackensack Medical Center, Northwell Health, John Radcliffe Hospital-Oxford.

**Postop relevance:** Wear duration matches average hospital stay. Used for in-hospital and post-discharge monitoring. Temperature monitoring is one of 8 parameters.

**Competitive threat level: HIGH** -- Well-funded, strong clinical adoption, directly relevant form factor.

---

### 2.2 Biobeat -- Cuffless BP Watch + Patch

**What it does:** Two form factors: disposable chest patch (short-term) and wrist monitor (long-term). PPG-based sensor continuously measures 13 health parameters including cuffless blood pressure, SpO2, pulse rate, respiratory rate, body temperature, stroke volume, cardiac output.

**FDA status:** Multiple 510(k) clearances including cuffless blood pressure, stroke volume, cardiac output.

**Revenue/Funding:** $71.8M total raised. $50M Series B (Dec 2025) for US expansion. Revenue: $17.6M (2024).

**Hospital adoption:** Nuvance Health, Tampa General Hospital, Northwell, Discovery Limited. Tampa General launched hospital-at-home program with Biobeat.

**Postop relevance:** Study of 114 post-surgical patients on unmonitored general care floor, wearing Biobeat sensor for first 72 hours post-PACU discharge. Specifically designed for postoperative vital signs screening.

**Competitive threat level: HIGH** -- Has specific postoperative clinical studies. Cuffless BP is major differentiator.

### 2.3 Caretaker Medical -- VitalStream

**What it does:** Wireless, wearable continuous hemodynamic monitor using finger cuff. Beat-by-beat continuous blood pressure (cNIBP), cardiac output, stroke volume, HRV, left ventricular ejection time. ICU-grade accuracy without invasive catheters.

**FDA status:** 510(k) cleared for continuous BP, cardiac output, stroke volume, advanced hemodynamics.

**Revenue/Funding:** $11.28M total raised. Distribution agreement with Terumo Health Outcomes (July 2025) for nationwide hospital distribution.

**Hospital adoption:** Available to VA medical centers. Terumo distribution deal expanding access. Piloted in cardiogenic shock monitoring.

**Postop relevance:** Perioperative hemodynamic monitoring is a specific use case. Detects hemodynamic instability -- one of the most dangerous postop complications. Wire-free monitoring through full mobile continuum including post-discharge.

**Competitive threat level: MEDIUM** -- Strong technology, early stage of commercial scale-up.

---

### 2.4 Sensium (The Surgical Company) -- Wireless Vitals Patch

**What it does:** Disposable chest-worn patch measuring HR, respiratory rate, temperature. Transmits wirelessly every 2 minutes. Push notifications to nurse's smartphone if vital sign deviates 7 consecutive readings (14 min). 5-day battery life. Waterproof.

**FDA status:** CE marked. Specific FDA status unclear from available data.

**Revenue/Funding:** Acquired by The Surgical Company (TSC) in 2016. Now operates as "TSC -- Connected Care." Spun out of Imperial College London.

**Hospital adoption:** Used in NHS hospitals (UK). Chelsea and Westminster Hospital deployed during COVID-19.

**Postop relevance:** DIRECTLY designed for postoperative monitoring on surgical wards. Clinical study: detected elevated respiratory rate 14 HOURS earlier and heart rate 2 HOURS earlier than nurses for post-GI surgery complications. Cost-utility analysis showed continuous monitoring was LESS COSTLY than intermittent monitoring alone (5,863 vs 6,329 GBP per patient). 69% patients felt safer; 74% positive experience.

**Competitive threat level: MEDIUM** -- Strongest postop-specific evidence but primarily European market.

### 2.5 Best Buy Health (formerly Current Health)

**What it does:** Wearable armband for continuous monitoring of 5 vital signs (pulse, SpO2, temperature, HR, respiratory rate). Combined with telehealth, patient engagement, and clinical dashboard. FDA-cleared continuous monitoring.

**FDA status:** FDA-cleared for continuous monitoring of 5 vital signs.

**Revenue/Funding:** Best Buy acquired Current Health for ~$400M (2021). Best Buy Health is a division of Best Buy ($43B company).

**Hospital adoption:** 14 clinical programs using ConnectedCare 365. Used for acute care at home, pre-surgical, post-acute transition, and chronic management. Partnership with Geisinger.

**Postop relevance:** Post-acute transitional care is a primary use case. Bridges hospital-to-home gap for surgical patients.

**Competitive threat level: MEDIUM-HIGH** -- Massive corporate backing, focused on hospital-at-home and transitional care.

## CATEGORY 3: AI/ML EARLY WARNING SYSTEMS FOR SEPSIS & DETERIORATION

### 3.1 Epic Systems -- Deterioration Index (DI) + Epic Sepsis Model (ESM)

**What it does:**
- **Deterioration Index (DI):** AI-driven algorithm embedded in Epic EHR. Scores 0-100 for probability of deterioration (RRT, resuscitation, ICU transfer, death) within 12-38 hours.
- **Epic Sepsis Model (ESM):** ML model for sepsis prediction, generates alerts within Epic workflow.

**FDA status:** Not separately FDA-cleared (considered clinical decision support within EHR).

**Revenue/Funding:** Epic Systems: ~$4.6B revenue (2024). Private company. No external funding needed.

**Hospital adoption:** DI deployed in "hundreds of hospitals." Epic holds 39.1% of US acute care hospital EHR market share. Score displayed on every admitted patient's chart at adopting hospitals.

**Postop relevance:** Runs automatically on ALL hospitalized patients including postop. However, controversial: external validation showed ESM had AUC of only 0.63 (vs. Epic's claimed performance), failed to identify 2/3 of sepsis patients, and generated alerts on 18% of all patients (alert fatigue). DI showed better performance for sepsis RRT prediction (100% specific, 73% sensitive at DI>40).

**Competitive threat level: VERY HIGH** -- Embedded in dominant EHR, automatically available to hundreds of hospitals. But performance is disputed.

---

### 3.2 Prenosis -- Sepsis ImmunoScore

**What it does:** AI/ML software that uses up to 22 inputs from EHR (including lab values and biomarkers) to generate sepsis risk score and assign patient to one of 4 risk categories. Predicts presence of or progression to sepsis within 24 hours.

**FDA status:** **First-ever FDA De Novo authorization for AI sepsis tool** (April 2024). Class II classification. Landmark regulatory event.

**Revenue/Funding:** $30M+ total raised from Pace Healthcare Capital and others. Revenue undisclosed. **Exclusive 5-year distribution deal with Roche** for US hospitals (April 2024).

**Hospital adoption:** Developed with 10 partner hospitals. 100,000+ blood samples from 25,000+ patients. Now commercially distributed via Roche's navify platform to thousands of hospitals.

**Postop relevance:** Directly applicable to postop sepsis detection. Intended for ED and hospital inpatients. The Roche distribution deal gives it massive commercial reach. First-mover advantage with FDA authorization.

**Competitive threat level: HIGH** -- First FDA-authorized AI sepsis tool + Roche distribution = formidable commercial position.

---

### 3.3 Dascena -- InSight (now CirrusDx)

**What it does:** ML algorithm that runs in background on EHR data, predicts sepsis up to 48 hours before symptoms appear. Computes risk by comparing patient data against millions of prior sepsis cases.

**FDA status:** FDA-approved.

**Revenue/Funding:** $55M total raised (led by Frazier Healthcare Partners). Revenue ~$2.5M. **Acquired and now operating as CirrusDx.**

**Hospital adoption:** Prospective study across 75,147 patient encounters at facilities from community hospitals to large academic centers. Showed 39.5% reduction in in-hospital mortality.

**Postop relevance:** Runs on all hospitalized patients. Strong clinical evidence for mortality reduction. However, company has been acquired/rebranded -- unclear current commercial momentum.

**Competitive threat level: MEDIUM** -- Strong clinical data but company appears to have lost independent momentum after acquisition.

---

### 3.4 CLEW Medical

**What it does:** AI-powered clinical surveillance platform. Originally ICU-focused (predicts respiratory failure and hemodynamic instability up to 8 hours in advance). **Expanded to ALL inpatient settings in April 2024** -- ED, med-surg floors, perioperative suites, specialty care. **Launched Sepsis Virtual Unit (SVU) in March 2025.**

**FDA status:** FDA-cleared (multiple). First FDA-cleared AI-based predictive models for high-acuity care.

**Revenue/Funding:** $31M raised (Series B). Based in Israel (Netanya).

**Hospital adoption:** Deployed in critical care departments across the US. Expanding to general ward settings.

**Postop relevance:** The April 2024 expansion to med-surg floors and perioperative suites makes this directly relevant. Claims 5x more accurate than other monitoring systems with 50x fewer alarms. Sepsis Virtual Unit specifically targets sepsis detection.

**Competitive threat level: HIGH** -- FDA-cleared, expanding from ICU to ward, strong accuracy claims.

### 3.5 PeraHealth / Spacelabs -- Rothman Index

### 3.6 Jvion -- CORE Platform

**What it does:** Prescriptive clinical AI that goes beyond prediction to recommend interventions. Identifies hidden patient risk across diseases/clinical events, and whether that risk trajectory can be changed. Covers sepsis, hospital-acquired infections, falls, pressure injuries.

**FDA status:** Not explicitly FDA-cleared as a medical device (clinical decision support).

**Revenue/Funding:** Private company. Specific funding not disclosed in search results.

**Hospital adoption:** Cleveland Clinic partnership for sepsis prevention. Southeast Health: 25.5% sepsis reduction, $442K-$741K monthly cost savings. Providers report average 30% reduction in preventable harm, $6.3M annual cost savings.

**Postop relevance:** Covers sepsis and other postop complications (falls, infections, pressure injuries). Prescriptive rather than just predictive -- recommends specific interventions.

**Competitive threat level: MEDIUM** -- Differentiated approach but less specific to postop monitoring.

## CATEGORY 5: NEWS/MEWS EARLY WARNING SCORES -- WHAT THEY ARE AND WHO AUTOMATES THEM

### What are NEWS/MEWS?

- **NEWS2 (National Early Warning Score 2):** Standardized scoring system (developed by UK Royal College of Physicians) that aggregates 6 vital signs (respiratory rate, SpO2, systolic BP, pulse rate, consciousness level, temperature) + supplemental oxygen status into a single composite score (0-20). Higher score = higher risk of deterioration. Triggers escalation protocols.
- **MEWS (Modified Early Warning Score):** Similar concept, slightly different parameters/weighting. Uses systolic BP, heart rate, respiratory rate, temperature, AVPU consciousness level.
- **Purpose:** Provide standardized, objective trigger for clinical escalation. Replace subjective nursing judgment with evidence-based scoring.

### Who automates it?

| Company | Product | Approach |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **Philips** | IntelliVue Guardian Solution | Auto-acquires vital signs, calculates EWS, alerts clinicians. Most mature automated EWS product. |
| **Capsule Technologies (Philips)** | EWSS | Bedside automated scoring integrated with any device manufacturer. |
| **GE HealthCare** | CARESCAPE Platform | Supports 7 EWS protocols (MEWS, NEWS2), 5 customizable profiles. |
| **Epic Systems** | Built-in EHR module | Auto-calculates from vital signs entered/imported into Epic. |
| **Cerner (Oracle Health)** | Built-in EHR module | Similar auto-calculation. |
| **Masimo** | Patient SafetyNet | Integrates modified EWS with continuous monitoring. |
| **Nihon Kohden** | SVM Series monitors | Early warning features for general wards. |

**Pricing:** Initial setup 30K-90K GBP per hospital, ongoing 0.35-0.70 GBP per acute bed per day.

**Key insight:** NEWS/MEWS automation is becoming table stakes -- most major monitoring and EHR companies include it. The differentiator is the DATA QUALITY going into the score (continuous vs. spot-check) and the AI LAYER on top.

## KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR NEOTHERM POSITIONING

### 1. The market is HUGE but the competition is INTENSE
Every major medical device company (Masimo, GE, Philips, Baxter, Medtronic) is actively pursuing continuous monitoring on surgical wards. This is not an empty space.

### 2. The trend is toward MULTI-PARAMETER continuous monitoring
Single-parameter monitoring (e.g., temperature only) is not where the market is going. Competitors measure 5-13 parameters simultaneously. A thermal-camera-only approach would need to demonstrate unique clinical value beyond what these multi-parameter systems already capture.

### 3. Temperature is already measured by most competitors
VitalConnect, Biobeat, Sensium, Best Buy Health, and many others already include continuous temperature as one of their monitored parameters. The question is whether THERMAL IMAGING provides something fundamentally different from contact temperature sensing.

### 4. The real opportunity gaps are:
- **Non-contact, zero-compliance monitoring** (only EarlySense/Baxter does this with their under-mattress sensor; thermal cameras would be truly contactless)
- **Spatial temperature mapping** (no competitor does full-body thermal mapping -- they measure at a single point)
- **Perfusion visualization** (thermal imaging can show regional perfusion changes that contact sensors cannot detect)
- **Wound/surgical site monitoring** (unique capability for thermal imaging -- no wearable can image a surgical wound)
- **Neonatal applications** (fragile skin, sensor intolerance -- thermal imaging has unique advantages)

### 5. AI sepsis prediction is well-funded and advancing fast
Prenosis (Roche-backed), Dascena, CLEW, Epic, Jvion all predict sepsis from EHR data. Thermal imaging would need to show it adds INCREMENTAL VALUE over these existing AI approaches, or provides earlier detection through a fundamentally different biological signal (peripheral vasoconstriction patterns visible on thermal before lab/vital sign changes).

### 6. Regulatory pathway is established
Multiple competitors have 510(k) clearances for continuous monitoring on general wards. The FDA De Novo pathway has been validated for AI sepsis tools (Prenosis). This is a well-trodden regulatory path.

### 7. Hospital adoption barriers are real but solvable
- Alert fatigue is the #1 concern (Epic Sepsis Model caused 43% increase in alerts during pandemic)
- Integration with existing EMR/workflows is critical
- Nursing buy-in required (Sensium: 63% nurses positive)
- Cost justification needed (but evidence shows continuous monitoring can be cost-saving -- Sensium study showed continuous monitoring was cheaper than intermittent)
