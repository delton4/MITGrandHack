2The Diagnostic Failure

Every tool today is too slow, too subjective, or too invasive

Neonatal sepsis signs are notoriously vague — overlapping with normal newborn physiology. The result: treat everyone empirically and deal with the consequences.

83–94%

of neonates treated for suspected sepsis have negative blood cultures. Nearly half receive prolonged antibiotics with zero evidence of infection.

Blood Culture

24–72 hour delay

Gold standard but requires blood volume neonates can barely spare. Often unavailable in LMICs.

CRP / Procalcitonin

6–24 hour delay

CRP has poor early sensitivity. PCT confounded by physiological surge in first 48h of life.

Capillary Refill Time

Highly subjective

Enormous inter-observer variability. Not standardized for pressure, duration, or measurement site.

WHO Clinical Signs (IMCI)

Low specificity

Sensitivity-optimized: catches cases but causes massive over-treatment. No POC test exists.

3The Discovery

A 2°C gap that predicts sepsis
before anything else

When sepsis begins, the body shunts blood centrally. The core stays warm while extremities cool. This core-peripheral temperature difference (cpTD) widens beyond 2°C — the earliest detectable clinical sign.

Details
Prospective observational study of 129 VLBW infants. cpTD >2°C sustained for 4 hours. Sensitivity 83%, NPV 94%. Thermal gradient alteration detected sepsis earlier than CRP or altered blood count in the majority of cases.
71%
of sepsis cases, cpTD was the first sign — before any lab or vital change
Leante-Castellanos 2017
Details
In 71% of confirmed late-onset sepsis episodes, the thermal gradient alteration was detected before any other clinical sign, laboratory marker, or vital sign change. This makes cpTD the single earliest indicator of developing sepsis.
<0.3°C
Mean absolute error of non-contact thermal cpTD vs. contact probes
NICU CPTD Study 2025, n=40
Details
Prospective study using thermal infrared camera with deep learning body parsing to auto-segment chest vs. limbs. Achieved MAE <0.3°C compared to clinical-grade contact thermometers. Fully automatic, continuous, non-contact monitoring proven feasible.
4Our Solution

A thermal eye for every incubator

NeoTherm is a low-cost thermal camera module that clips onto any NICU incubator and continuously monitors the temperature gap that signals sepsis.

01

Mount

Clip module onto incubator. CaF2 IR window enables imaging through walls.

02

Segment

Deep learning auto-detects chest (core) and foot (peripheral) in real-time.

03

Compute

Continuously calculates cpTD and tracks trends over time.

04

Alert

When cpTD > 2°C threshold persists, nurses receive an immediate alert.
