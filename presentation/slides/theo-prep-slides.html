Source — Sepsis

Leante-Castellanos et al. 2012

J Perinatal Medicine. 2012;40(5):571–576. PubMed 22945277
Sensitivity: “90.9% [95% CI, 62.3–98.4].”
Specificity: “90% (95% CI, 69.9–97.2%).”

“In 80% of cases, it [thermal gradient alteration] occurred before abnormal laboratory findings.”
31 VLBW infants. CPTD >2°C detected sepsis before labs in 4 out of 5 cases.
P1
Source — Sepsis

Leante-Castellanos et al. 2017

Pediatr Infect Dis J. 2017;36(12):e293–e297. PubMed 28719503
“Adjusted odds ratio for late-onset sepsis of 23.60 (95% CI, 6.80–81.88).”

Sensitivity: 83%. Negative predictive value: 94%.

“Thermal gradient alteration was the first clinical sign of sepsis” in 71% of cases.
CRP was <1.5 mg/dL in 64% at time of detection.
129 VLBW infants over 52 months. The largest prospective CPTD-for-sepsis study.
P2
Source — Sepsis

Ussat et al. 2015

Early Hum Dev. 2015;91(12):677–681. PubMed 26513628
“cpTD was the most striking clinical sign associated with both Clin- (adOR 6.3) and Prov-LOS (adOR 10.5).”

CPTD >2°C: OR 9 for clinical LOS, OR 16 for culture-proven LOS.
83 episodes in 67 infants. Independent German replication.
P3
Source — Automated CPTD

Zhang et al. 2025

Biomedical Optics Express. 2025;16(3):965–981. PMC11919349
“Non-contact, fully automatic, and continuous CPTD measurement for neonates.”

40 preterm infants. AggPose Vision Transformer. MAE < 0.3°C.

“Infants with circulatory disorders exhibited elevated CPTD values.”
Hands showed “more pronounced response to changes in core temperature” than feet.
First automated camera-based CPTD in a real NICU. Proves the measurement layer.
P4
Source — Multimodal Sepsis

Lungu et al. 2024

Bioengineering. 2024;11(7):681. PMC11273471
121 newborns (39 LOS, 35 EOS, 47 controls). AUC 0.88. Accuracy 87.67%.

Feature importance: NIRS ~0.90, skin temperature ~0.85, SpO2 ~0.75.

“Near-infrared spectroscopy and skin temperature had the greatest impact on model accuracy” — 40% improvement over single modalities.
Skin temperature outperforms pulse oximetry for sepsis prediction.
P5
Source — Alarm Management

Yang et al. 2024

Comput Methods Programs Biomed. 2024;255:108335. PubMed 39047574
119 preterm (<32 wk). Best model AUC 0.875 at 6h before deterioration.

Detected 96.1% of sepsis cases before clinical crash.

Multi-threshold alarm: “an average of 1.15 alarms per day per patient,” 71.6% sensitivity.
Alarm fatigue is solvable: ~1 alarm/patient/day catches 72% of events.
P6
Source — Shock Prediction

Vats et al. 2022

Front Physiol. 2022;13:862411. PMC9340772
22 PICU patients. 103,936 thermal frames. LSTM on CPD time series.

“Best area under the receiver operating characteristic curve of 0.81 ± 0.06 and area under the precision-recall curve of 0.78 ± 0.05 at 5 h,” providing clinicians adequate time for early intervention.

Domain features (CPD time series) outperformed direct image classification.
Only study combining thermal video + DL + CPD for prospective shock prediction.
P7
Source — HeRO Precedent

Moorman et al. 2011 (HeRO RCT)

J Pediatrics. 2011;159(6):900–906. PMC3215822
“Two-group, parallel, individually randomized controlled clinical trial of 3,003 very low birth weight infants in 9 NICUs.”

“Mortality decreased from 10.2% to 8.1%” — HR 0.78 (95% CI 0.61–0.99, P = 0.04).

Number needed to monitor: 48 (all infants); 23 (ELBW).

“Heart rate characteristics monitoring can reduce mortality in very low birth weight infants.”
Proved: continuous biomarker display → earlier action → 22% mortality reduction.
P8
Source — Shock

Nagori et al. 2019

Scientific Reports. 2019;9:91. PubMed 30643187
539 thermal images, 253 with concurrent intra-arterial BP.

Automated ROI segmentation: “96% agreement with a human expert.”

AUC: “75% at 0 hours (classification), 77% at 3 hours (prediction) and 69% at 12 hours.”
First proof: thermal imaging + ML predicts shock hours ahead.

Source — NEC

Rice et al. 2010

J Surgical Radiology. 2010. ResearchGate
FLIR SC640 camera in ELBW infants in heated incubators.

NEC infants had significantly lower abdominal surface temperatures: 35.3°C vs 36.6°C in controls.
1.3°C difference — well within sensitivity of modern cameras (NETD <50mK).

Source — NEC

Ntonfo et al. 2015

IEEE MeMeA 2015. IEEE 7145168
NEC infants showed statistically higher thermal asymmetry across the abdomen compared to controls.

Left/right abdominal temperature analysis differentiates inflamed vs. normal bowel regions.
First quantitative evidence: L/R thermal asymmetry distinguishes NEC from controls.

Source — NEC

Shi et al. 2020 (CHEO Ottawa)

IEEE MeMeA 2020. IEEE 9137344
Integrated system combining infrared thermal imaging with RGB-D sensing. Automatic body segmentation and temperature distribution analysis across the abdominal region without manual intervention.

“Encouraging initial results” comparing NEC vs. normal thermal distributions.
Most technically advanced NEC thermal detection system. Active research program.

Source — Respiratory

Maurya et al. 2023

J Clin Monit Comput. 2022. PMC10175339
Deep-learning tracking-by-detection (YOLO5Face) + RGB-thermal fusion.

MAE: ~1.5 breaths/min. “About 80% of estimates showing error less than 2 breaths/minute.”

“A clinically relevant alternative to the contact-based method.”
Best-in-class neonatal respiratory rate accuracy.
P13
Source — Respiratory & Apnea

Lorato et al. 2020 / 2021

Biomed Opt Express. 2020;11(9):4848. PMC7510882
“Respiration is monitored in neonatal wards using chest impedance (CI), which is obtrusive and can cause skin damage. Therefore, unobtrusive solutions based on infrared thermography are being investigated.”

3 FLIR Lepton cameras. MAE: 2.07 breaths/min.
2021 follow-up: apnea classification — obstructive vs. central: F1 0.86–0.93.
$200 cameras. Clinical-grade accuracy. Chest impedance cannot classify apnea type.

Source — Heart Rate + Respiratory

Khanam et al. 2021

J Imaging. 2021;7(8):122. PMC8404938
7 NICU infants. RGB camera (iPPG, green channel).

HR: “MAE 1.80 beats/min, RMSE 2.23, Pearson r = 0.9864.”
RR: “MAE 2.13 breaths/min, RMSE 2.69, r = 0.9453.”
Simultaneous non-contact HR + RR in actual NICU infants.

Source — Thermoregulation

Urisarri et al. 2021

Nature Communications. 2021;12:5274. Nature Comms
IRT confirmed as a valid method for detecting brown adipose tissue activation in neonates via interscapular thermography. Validated against indirect calorimetry.
Nature Communications validation. BAT = metabolic stress marker for cold stress, hypoglycemia.

Source — HIE / Brain

Walas et al. 2021

Scientific Reports. 2021;11:19789.
Novel “Thermal Index” derived from fontanelle thermal imaging during therapeutic hypothermia. Validated on 6 neonates with HIE.

Thermal Index correlated with MRI-confirmed brain injury patterns.
Fontanelle = unique thermal window to the brain. Non-invasive brain injury monitoring.

Source — Pain & Stress

Kretschmer et al. 2023

Pediatric Research. 2023. Ped Research
Nasal tip shows the greatest temperature change in hungry vs. not-hungry infants.

Autonomic stress responses change facial skin blood flow — detectable thermally.
Essentially unstudied area. Same camera could enable objective pain/stress assessment.
