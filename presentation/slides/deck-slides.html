The Problem

Neonatal sepsis kills.
Current monitoring misses it.

Adhesive contact probes damage premature skin. Single-point measurement. Must be manually repositioned.
Spot checks every 1–4 hours. Sepsis develops between readings. By the time vital signs change, the inflammatory cascade is already underway.
No spatial temperature data. A single axillary probe cannot detect the core-peripheral gradient shift that precedes clinical sepsis signs.
Late detection = worse outcomes. Each hour of delayed antibiotic treatment in neonatal sepsis increases mortality risk.

The Clinical Insight

The body signals sepsis through temperature gradients

CPTD = Tcore − Tperipheral
Core-Peripheral Temperature Difference. In sepsis, sympathetic vasoconstriction cools extremities while the core remains stable.
Normal
< 2°C
Sepsis Marker
> 2°C
sustained ≥ 4 hours
Leante-Castellanos et al. (2012, 2017): CPTD >2°C for ≥4h — sensitivity 83%, NPV 94%, first clinical sign in 71% of cases.
Zhang et al. (2025): Automated CPTD via thermal camera, MAE 0.297°C in 40 preterm infants.
